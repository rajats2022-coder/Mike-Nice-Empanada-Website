# Mike Nice Empanadas — Food Truck Merch + Printify Research

## What food/restaurant merch tends to sell
- Restaurant/food-truck merch works when it feels like a brand/community item, not a random logo slap.
- Common proven categories: t-shirts, sweatshirts/hoodies, hats, aprons, totes, mugs, stickers.
- Aprons are especially aligned for food brands because they connect to cooking/prep and staff wear.
- Apparel should be wearable in public: clean logo hits, tasteful slogans, good blanks, no clutter.
- Low-risk launch pattern: start with 3–5 products, validate interest locally/socially, then publish winners.

## Recommended first drop
1. Black logo tee / hero tee — strongest local fan item.
2. Cream logo tee — safer broad seller, readable logo.
3. Black hoodie — higher-margin cold-weather / loyal-fan item.
4. Black apron — most food-brand aligned and staff-friendly.
5. Canvas tote — useful for markets/pop-ups/catering pickup.
6. Sticker add-on — cheap impulse item, good with every order.

## Design QA rules going forward
- Use realistic fabric/product mockups, not flat SVG clothing shapes.
- Keep logo centered, proportional, and readable.
- Avoid black artwork on black garments unless backed by cream/light patch.
- Do not invent weak slogans unless Rajat/Mike approves them.
- Validate mockup images visually before putting them on the site.
